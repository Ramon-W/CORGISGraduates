Overview

The Graduates dataset¹ is based off of the National Survey of Recent College Graduates². The dataset includes multiple surveys, each in a different year. The time gap between surveys are not constant, but is generally around 2-3 years. It only surveys those who have graduated with their degree in at least the last 2 academic years, those who are under 73 years old, and only those that are graduates of a U.S. academic institution. It sorts the data by year, and then by major. This dataset is especially useful to determine trends in education, employment opportunities, and salaries of graduate students.

Commonly Asked Questions

  • Why are Asians seperated from other minorities in this dataset? Generally, Asians outnumber the combined total of other minority groups in each major, so seperating them would result in more useful data.
  • What is the difference between “Not in the Labor Force” and “Unemployed”? According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics³, “Unemployed” is meant for people who are still looking for jobs while "Not in the Labor Force" relates to people who are neither in a job nor looking for one.
  • Why are there only 0.0s for the data in some majors? If a major (in a certain year) has only 0.0s, it means the major had not existed yet in that year.
